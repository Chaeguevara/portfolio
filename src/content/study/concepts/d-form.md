# D-Form

*(한국어: [D-폼 (D-Form)](/portfolio/study/d-form.ko/))*

> A smooth convex surface made by gluing the perimeters of two convex curves of equal length.

## Idea
A **D-form** is built by taking **two convex shapes** (e.g. two ellipses or disks)
of equal perimeter and gluing their boundaries together edge-to-edge. The result —
by the smooth version of [Alexandrov's Theorem](/portfolio/study/alexandrov-theorem/) — is a unique closed **smooth convex
surface**. Introduced by the artist Tony Wills.

## Family
- **Pita form:** glue the two halves of a *single* convex curve's perimeter to each
  other (one shape).
- **D-form:** glue *two* convex curves (the main case).
- **Seam form:** glue arbitrarily many, not-necessarily-convex pieces — needs the
  smooth Alexandrov–Pogorelov version.

## Why it matters
D-forms are the smooth/curved counterpart of folding polygons into polyhedra, and a
striking example of [Alexandrov's Theorem](/portfolio/study/alexandrov-theorem/) in action. They have neat provable
properties (e.g. the seam is planar / lies on the convex hull) and connect folding
to industrial design and sculpture.
