# Hinged Dissection

*(한국어: [경첩 분할 (Hinged Dissection)](/portfolio/study/hinged-dissection.ko/))*

> A chain of polygons hinged at vertices that can refold to form any of several target shapes; such universal dissections always exist.

## Idea
A **hinged dissection** cuts a shape into pieces connected in a chain by hinges, so
that swinging the pieces about the hinges reassembles them into a *different* target
shape — and the same chain can do several targets. The classic image: a chain of
polygons that folds into a triangle, then a square, etc.

## Why it matters
A striking existence theorem: for **any** finite set of polygons of equal area,
there is a single hinged chain that folds into each of them. It ties the design of
mechanisms to classical dissection geometry, with playful applications (animated
furniture, transforming tables).

## Connections to linkage folding
- **Foldability side:** chains with **slender adornments** (thin shapes hung on a
  chain) cannot lock — a generalization of the [Carpenter's Rule Theorem](/portfolio/study/carpenters-rule-theorem/) via
  expansiveness.
- Some configurations *do* lock (using the infinitesimally-locked theory of
  [Locked Linkage](/portfolio/study/locked-linkage/)s, "Rules 1 and 2").
- 3D/4D (non-hinged) dissection is governed by the [Dehn Invariant](/portfolio/study/dehn-invariant/).
