# Uniaxial Base

*(한국어: [단축 베이스 (Uniaxial Base)](/portfolio/study/uniaxial-base.ko/))*

> A folded base whose flaps all lie perpendicular to a single line (axis), so its shadow is exactly a tree.

## Idea
A **uniaxial base** is the intermediate folded form targeted by the
[Tree Method (Origami Design)](/portfolio/study/tree-method/). All the paper's flaps hang perpendicular to one common axis line,
so projecting (the "shadow") collapses the base onto a **tree** — the same metric
tree you started the design from. Each flap becomes a tree edge of matching length.

## Why it matters
"Uniaxial" is the precise property that makes the tree method work: it is the bridge
between the abstract metric tree (what shape you want) and the concrete crease
pattern (how to fold it). Most classic complex-origami bases are uniaxial.

## Details
- The **active paths** (taut paths between leaf circles in the packing) map to the
  axis-parallel ridge creases.
- Regions between paths are filled by [Universal Molecule](/portfolio/study/universal-molecule/)s so the whole CP folds
  flat and respects the tree.
- Generalizing beyond uniaxial bases is part of what makes design harder and more
  artistic.
